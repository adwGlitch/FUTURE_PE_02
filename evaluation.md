# Prompt Evaluation Matrix: Reminderr Campaign

This document evaluates the output quality generated by the three versions of our prompts (Prompt V1, Prompt V2, and the Final Prompt). The comparison highlights how prompt engineering techniques directly affected content quality and performance.

---

## 1. Evaluation Matrix

Each prompt version was graded on a scale of **1 (Poor)** to **5 (Exceptional)** across six core creative benchmarks.

| Evaluation Criterion | Prompt V1 | Prompt V2 | Final Prompt | Critical Observations |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **Hook Quality** | 2/5 | 4/5 | **5/5** | V1 used generic hooks like "Do you want to be productive?". V2 and Final used relatable, highly localized questions and problems. |
| **Brand Consistency** | 3/5 | 4/5 | **5/5** | V1 sounded like a corporate sales pitch. Final prompt aligned perfectly with Reminderr's "friendly, minimal, supportive" voice. |
| **CTA Effectiveness** | 2/5 | 3/5 | **5/5** | V1 CTAs were generic ("Buy now"). Final prompt generated 20 distinct CTAs grouped by intent and low-friction parameters. |

---

## 2. Key Improvements Explained

### From Prompt V1 to Prompt V2: Tone & Voice
*   **What Improved:** The language became less corporate and more conversational. Scripts transitioned from sounding like a television advertisement to a person talking to their front-facing phone camera.
*   **Why Change Was Made:** Prompt V1 lacked negative constraints. Without banning words like "revolutionary" and "cutting-edge," the AI defaulted to generic, sales-heavy marketing tropes.
*   **Method Used:** Applied Persona prompting ("expert UGC creator") and negative list constraints (banned words).

### From Prompt V2 to Final Prompt: Format, Platform & Production Value
*   **What Improved:** Added detailed storyboards (camera angles, visuals) for all 10 scripts, 40 short-form platform variants, and clear markdown tables.
*   **Why Change Was Made:** Prompt V2 only generated the script dialog. In a real agency workflow, creators need visual guidelines (storyboards) and media buyers need platform-specific copy (TikTok vs. YouTube Shorts require different hooks and pacing).
*   **Method Used:** Structured templates, zero-shot layouts, and explicit multi-platform segmentation.

---

## 3. Final Observations
The evaluation proves that **constraints and structured templates** are the most powerful levers in prompt engineering. By telling the AI what *not* to say and providing the exact Markdown structure to fill in, we eliminated the typical "AI signature" and generated output that looks like it was written by a real performance marketing agency.
